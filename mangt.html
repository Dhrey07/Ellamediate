A co-ownership is a legal structure that allows multiple investors to jointly own real estate or a portfolio of assets by pooling resources and sharing the risks of real estate acquisition and development.

TYPES OF CO-OWNERSHIP

There are two main types of co-ownership: Tenancy In Common and Joint Tenancy. Every kind of co-ownership provides different rights and obligations as follows;

Tenancy In Common
Tenant in common has a separate fractional interest in the entire property but may also possess and use the whole property. Tenants In Common may hold unequal interest in the property based on their contributions.

Joint Tenancy
The joint tenants own the entire property as a single united interest. The property must be divided equally among the joint tenants. Each joint tenant has the right to occupy the whole property, except for the rights of the other joint tenant (s).

Community Property
Community property comprises assets that come into marriage through any means other than inheritance or gift. Assets obtained by the husband or wife are recognised as assets of the marital community, regardless of their title.

Community Property
Tenancy by the entirety is a type of shared ownership of property recognised in most states, available only to married couples. Spouses who hold property as tenants by the whole have an undivided interest in the property, similar to a joint tenancy.
